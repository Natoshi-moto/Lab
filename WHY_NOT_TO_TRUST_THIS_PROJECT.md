## Permanent reasons not to trust this project

### A. Who is driving (always true)

1. **The operator is anonymous.** There is no public, verified human identity you can subpoena, hire, sue, or rely on as a long-term maintainer-of-record for product safety.

2. **The operator cannot write the code.** Implementation is performed by replaceable AI seats and occasional tooling — not by a human who can personally guarantee what shipped.

3. **The operator does not have computer-science training.** They cannot independently audit cryptography, browser security models, supply chains, or distributed systems. “Operator accepted” means **go/no-go judgment**, not expert verification.

4. **The operator’s main controls are approve/reject and paste packets.** That is real power over process and zero power over subtle incorrectness that looks green.

5. **Anonymity + non-expertise is a structural accountability hole.** Even perfect code does not create a responsible party you know how to find.

6. **The operator can disappear.** Anonymous projects die when attention dies. No SLA, no company, no guaranteed succession.

7. **The operator can be socially engineered.** Because they depend on AI narratives and chat interfaces, a confident false summary can move `main` as easily as a true one if gates are weak.

### B. What AI seats are (always true)

8. **AI seats are not independent auditors.** Same-lab multi-model agreement is **not** independent corroboration (`AI_ROUTING.md`). Three AIs nodding is still one epistemic culture plus correlated training priors.

9. **AI seats hallucinate, omit, and overclaim.** Receipts reduce this; they do not eliminate it.

10. **AI seats are replaceable and non-liable.** No model vendor is your fiduciary because a seat wrote a bridge fix.

11. **AI seats optimize for coherent stories.** Coherence is not correctness. Eloquence has already failed this Lab before; executable invariants matter more.

12. **Tooling can lie or be mis-scoped.** Green CI, green `ods:p0`, green typecheck — each proves only what it was written to prove.

13. **The humans reading AI output may not catch the miss.** Especially when the only human in the loop is non-technical by design.

### C. What the Lab is (always true)

14. **`status_authority: NONE` is constitutional, not temporary.** Nothing in this repo promotes itself to court-grade or bank-grade truth by existing here.

15. **Public repository ≠ reviewed release.** Visibility was resolved public; that increases exposure of mistakes, not confidence.

16. **Receipts are lab slips, not warranties.** A receipt records claims and non-claims under a seat; it is not insurance.

17. **History is messy and multi-branch.** Worktrees, draft PRs, local-only commits, and parallel design branches mean “what I pulled” may not be “what you think is canonical” unless you pin hashes.

18. **CANONICAL_AS_IS is not “correct.”** Frozen snapshots preserve state; they do not bless ethics, security, or product-market honesty.

19. **The Lab mixes research programmes.** Beneficial Genesis, PCX, Noted, papers, audits — cross-reading can create false confidence that one programme’s rigor covers another.

### D. What Noted / host software is (always true even if hardened)

20. **It is research software, not a product contract.** No support desk, no formal threat model as a paid deliverable, no certified evaluation.

21. **Local-first is a design intent, not a mathematical proof of isolation.** Browser platforms, OS APIs, extensions, backups, and screen capture exist outside Lab doctrine.

22. **Embedded third-party and model-provider paths reintroduce trust in outsiders.** Anytime keys, prompts, or scripts leave the machine (CDN, API, proxy), “your machine only” is false for that flow.

23. **Dependency ecosystems are hostile by default.** `npm` trees, transitive CVEs, install scripts, and lockfile drift are permanent classes of risk — remediating today’s five findings does not end the class.

24. **Browser security is a moving target.** What “sandbox” and “same-origin” mean in practice changes with engines and flags; a green test on one Chrome build is not forever.

25. **Electron or desktop shells, if used, add OS-level blast radius.** Whether shipping or dormant, the *class* of risk remains whenever a shell can open external URLs or touch the filesystem.

26. **UI polish is orthogonal to trustworthiness.** Layout density and pretty First Contact do not secure storage, keys, or bridges.

27. **Scrubbing personal seed data ≠ security audit.** Removing test sessions is hygiene, not a penetration test.

### E. Economics, law, and social claims (always true)

28. **Nothing here authorizes real-world economic value.** Checkpoint 001 and non-claims forbid treating tips, ranks, NEX-like strings, or run IDs as money. If someone does anyway, that is their break with doctrine — and still not the project’s license to be a financial product.

29. **This is not legal advice, investment advice, or medical advice.** Ever.

30. **Synthetic “economy” language is easy to misread as a token pitch.** The project must fight that misreading forever; the risk of misreading never goes to zero.

31. **No KYC, no entity, no insurance.** You cannot treat this as a counterparty.

32. **Jurisdiction is undefined for anonymous research.** If something goes wrong, there may be no clear venue or responsible organization.

### F. Epistemic break points (always true)

33. **You cannot outsource responsibility for your secrets.** If you paste API keys, personal notes, or seed phrases into this stack, the failure is yours even if a bug helped.

34. **Passing adversarial campaigns does not mint trust.** At best it narrows known failure modes under a non-claim.

35. **The project can look alive while being unmaintainable.** Active PRs and multi-seat theater are not proof of a viable long-term product.

36. **Breakage is information.** If the next phase cannot survive the stop-the-line gates, the distrust register, and an honest cold-drop bar, **the correct outcome is failure in public** — not a quieter demo.

37. **“It works on my machine” is operator-local.** Anonymous remote strangers do not share your disk, your browser profile, or your AI chat context.

38. **Future you is a stranger.** Without pins, receipts, and this distrust register, even the operator should not trust memory of what was “accepted.”
