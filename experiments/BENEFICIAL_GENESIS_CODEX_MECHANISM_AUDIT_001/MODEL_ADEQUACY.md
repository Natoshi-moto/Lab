# Model adequacy

| Model/claim | Endogenous but treated as input | Omitted feedback/market | What is established |
|---|---|---|---|
| Allocation | participation, denominator, claim composition | entry/exit after expected dilution | arithmetic mapping only |
| Rebate | access, enforcement, charity selection | reputation, detection, repeated contracting | conditional incidence/threshold |
| Tainted source | opportunity cost, recoverability, enforcement | claims, freezes, charity disposal | pathway and conditional sign |
| Governance | turnout, delegation, coalition, exchange aggregation | vote markets and secondary accumulation | structural mappings under chosen rules |
| Transfer/lockup | demand and liquidity | price discovery, exit, speculation feedback | availability/timing, not welfare magnitude |
| Welfare | additional giving, displacement, retention | downstream charity efficacy | conditional sign regions |

Utilities are illustrative and linear. Probabilities are unmeasured. Secondary markets, donor-charity matching, cutoff-miner markets, exchange internal accounting, and adaptive participation are omitted. Structural pathways establish possibility; they do not establish empirical direction, magnitude, threshold frequency, or inevitability.

No synthetic parameter is called realistic. More synthetic simulation cannot measure participation, displacement, rebate access, tainted-flow incidence, liquidity, charity efficacy, or legal treatment.
