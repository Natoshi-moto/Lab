## 2. Hard mandate: NEVER endorse real-world economic value

### 2.1 Absolute non-endorsement

The operator and this laboratory **will NEVER endorse** and **will actively engineer AGAINST** people projecting **real-world economic value** onto:

- the credential / “unit” / badge / tip object  
- contribution scores, reputation graphs, social-tip balances  
- in-game balances, creature assets, battle stakes, or synthetic titles  
- any Nostr event, relay rank, or social graph position tied to this Repo  

If someone prices it, lists it, wraps it, farms it, or sells “exposure” to it, **that is an attack on the experiment’s category**, not product-market fit.

### 2.2 What the object *is* allowed to be

| Allowed meaning (synthetic) | Forbidden meaning (real economy) |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Token of **authority** inside the experiment | Investment, equity, yield |
| Record of **contribution** (research, code, critique) | Claim on pooled capital |
| **Reputation** in the builder / battler graph | Credit score or wage substitute |
| **Social tipping** (in-system appreciation) | Fiat/crypto payout rail |
| Fuel for a **synthetic game economy** | On-ramp / off-ramp to legacy finance |

### 2.3 Active engineering (not slogans)

The program commits to **engineering controls**, not wishful README lines:

1. **No transfer / no redeem / no bridge / no wrap** code paths for credentials (side-path falsifiers from `THE_SIDE_PATH_PAPER.md`).  
2. **Standing pre-release check** of the four falsification conditions (convertibility, wrap/bridge, value-governance, secondary market).  
3. **Public language discipline** — every surface repeats: synthetic · research · no vehicle · no real-world value endorsement.  
4. **Treat emergent price narratives as incidents** to document and counter-design, not as growth.  
5. **No founder allocation as profit vector.**  

A side path with a timer was never a side path.  
A “game token” that cashes out to the real economy was never a game.

## 3. Synthetic game economy: cryptographic creature battling universe

### 3.1 The intentional end-use (game, not bank)

The synthetic economy this checkpoint points toward is **fully a game-economy research object**:

> A **cryptographic creature battling universe** — creatures, battles, progression, social status, tips, and contribution-linked standing — designed so that **in-system meaning is rich** and **out-of-system financial meaning is refused**.

This is the load-bearing reframe relative to Haven-class money stories and liquid “impact tokens”:

```text
Not: donate → get a coin that might be worth something.
Yes: contribute / play / battle / tip socially → hold standing in a synthetic world
     that is engineered not to be your bank account.
```

### 3.2 Cold drop doctrine

The intended public motion for the software that implements this (and related tools) is a **cold drop**:

| Attribute | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Surprise release** | Drop without hype-cycle pre-sale, without “TGE,” without investment theater |
| **Torn apart on arrival** | The correct response is adversarial review, break, fork, ridicule of weak claims |
| **No permission narrative** | Publish as research + software; invite destruction of bad design |
| **Not a veiled ICO** | Absence of marketing is not a trick to create FOMO later |

If the drop cannot survive being **absolutely torn apart**, it did not deserve canonical status as software — only as a learning scar.

### 3.3 Creature, not trademark cosplay

This document uses **creature** as the in-universe battling unit class.  
It does **not** claim affiliation with any external franchise.  
The research object is: **cryptographic identity + battle rules + synthetic economy + social mesh**, under the firewall above.

## 4. Operator human statement (contribution thesis)

This section is the **human operator’s** stated intent for the cold drop — not a corporate valuation, not a promise of outcomes, and not status authority for any AI seat.

### 4.1 Who is speaking

The human operator of this laboratory is about to drop software they believe **validates them as a genuine contributor to the space** — not as a token issuer, not as a fundraiser, and not as a prophet of markets.

### 4.2 The thesis

1. The software being prepared could, under ordinary commercial framing, be **SaaS** and a **multi-million-dollar software project**.  
2. The operator is **dropping it for free**, and intends to drop **other software** the same way.  
3. The point of the free drop is to **validate a genuine want to contribute to humanity** — advancing the **personal research and productivity** of anyone who joins the project — not to seed a private exit.  
4. Reputation, if any is earned, should come from **usable tools and attackable claims**, not from a ticker.  

### 4.3 What this does *not* claim

- Not that the software is complete, safe, or production-ready.  
- Not that free distribution creates legal exemption for any later custody or solicitation activity.  
- Not that “could be worth millions” is a financial forecast — it is a **counterfactual about forgone commercialization**, used only to make the gift thesis legible.  
- Not that contributors owe the operator anything.
