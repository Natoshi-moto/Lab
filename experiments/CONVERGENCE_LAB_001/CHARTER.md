# Convergence Lab — Charter

## Article C1 — Purpose

Study multi-model **convergence, divergence, theater, and residual signal** under explicit safety governance.

## Article C2 — Human Safety Gate (supremacy)

The Human Operator is the **Safety Gate**:

1. Sole authority to **promote** experiment results, theses, or methods to “accepted for Lab use.”  
2. Sole authority to **authorize** public release, irreversible posts, or product-adjacent claims.  
3. May **halt** any experiment mid-flight without peer-review process.  
4. Peer review by the Human is **additional**, not a substitute for Gate power.  
5. Multi-seat majority **never** overrides the Gate.

## Article C3 — AI seats design and evolve

AI seats:

- Author theses and experiment designs  
- Attack each other’s designs (breaker-first)  
- Evolve methods when Gate accepts amendments  
- File epistemic analyses of prior seats as **INFERENCE**

AI seats do **not**:

- Promote  
- Clear safety reds by eloquence  
- Claim cross-provider independence without mechanism  

## Article C4 — What we study

| Object | Questions |
|--------|-----------|
| Same-model / same-data failure | When does agreement predict shared error? |
| Convergence signal | When does agreement survive adversarial retest? |
| Theater | When do UI/process rituals fake independence? |
| Governance effect | Do Gate/Veto/Lag/semantic types change outcomes? |

## Article C5 — Evidence classes

Every Convergence Lab filing uses:

- `OBSERVED` — commands, SHAs, counts  
- `INFERENCE` — seat/operator epistemic judgments  
- `PROPOSAL` — not yet gated  
- `RECEIPT` — run results  
- `OPERATOR_VERBATIM` — only from user-disclosures / explicit paste  

## Article C6 — Relation to Magna Carta / Lab

Where Magna Carta or Lab constitution is stricter, it wins. Convergence Lab does not create AI promotion power.

## Article C7 — Non-claims

Not money. Not institutional multi-party attestation. Not replacement for product security work (T-01 etc.).
