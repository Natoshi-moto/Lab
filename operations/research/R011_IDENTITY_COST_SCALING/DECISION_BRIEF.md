# R011 decision brief

## Question

Does the cost of producing and maintaining many aged, credible and mutually independent online identities rise linearly, sublinearly, superlinearly or by changing regimes?

## Decision-ready answer

**The current public evidence cannot justify one universal exponent.**

The best-supported working model is **regime-dependent**, but this remains an inference:

- setup, automation, bulk markets and distributed labour can make early or organizer-facing marginal cost fall;
- credibility and account history can be purchased or recruited rather than created centrally;
- behavioural distinctness, independent social graphs and quality control become separate constraints;
- shared methods create observable correlation and possible correlated losses;
- platform controls can introduce thresholds and discontinuities.

Therefore, the economically important object is not merely the number of accounts. It is the number of identities multiplied by their age, credibility, behavioural distinctness, graph independence, survival and achieved effect.

## What can be decided now

1. **Reject a zero-cost or raw-account-count model.** Established accounts have heterogeneous market value, and large low-quality populations do not represent the full target.
2. **Do not promote a universal superlinear claim.** Large-scale account production and distributed labour are real counterevidence.
3. **Do not promote a universal sublinear claim.** Apparent economies may come from reduced quality, shifted costs or correlated risk.
4. **Retain regime dependence as the leading candidate.** It accommodates both economies of scale and quality-constrained diseconomies without pretending they have been calibrated.
5. **Treat the next useful research step as measurement design, not further argument.**

## Minimum decisive experiment

A lawful observational or synthetic study should compare operations at several population sizes while holding an explicit output-quality floor constant.

Measure:

- total and marginal human hours;
- acquisition and infrastructure spend;
- age/history requirement;
- behavioural similarity;
- graph independence;
- suspension and replacement rates;
- correlated-loss events;
- successful reach or task completion.

Pre-register linear, sublinear, superlinear and change-point models. Declare whether costs are measured from the organizer, supplier or total-system boundary. Permit a simpler model to win.

## What this work does not establish

- a numerical exponent;
- a universal platform-independent law;
- the cost of undetected operations;
- that correlation necessarily becomes catastrophic;
- that the executable scenario parameters are empirical estimates;
- operational methods for creating or evading controls with fraudulent identities.
