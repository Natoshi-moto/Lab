# R011 hard vertical slice — execution route

## Mission

Produce a defensible, falsifiable answer to whether the cost of fabricating and maintaining many independent, aged, credible online identities is linear, superlinear or regime-dependent, while testing Nexus as a low-friction multi-seat research workflow.

## Default operating rule

Continue without asking the user for routine choices. Escalate only a consequential preference, a new contradiction, a local/credential dependency, or canonical promotion.

## Seats

### 1. Evidence seat

Collect public, lawful, external evidence from primary research, official reports, credible datasets and clearly identified secondary synthesis. Record provenance, date, scope, limitations and relevance. Evidence submission has no promotion authority.

### 2. Mechanism seat

Construct competing models for identity creation, ageing, maintenance, behavioural coherence, relationship-graph formation, detection pressure and correlated failure. Label assumptions explicitly. Candidate models have no evidentiary authority merely because they are mathematically coherent.

### 3. Falsification seat

Attempt to defeat every scaling claim. Search for cheap automation, economies of scale, bottleneck sharing, correlated compromise, platform heterogeneity and threshold effects. Review has `status_authority: NONE`.

### 4. Engineering seat

Build a small inspectable computational artifact that can represent competing scaling regimes and expose sensitivity to assumptions. Tests demonstrate implementation properties only.

### 5. Coordinator seat

Maintain the evidence/inference boundary, route context, reconcile disputes without erasing minority positions, track unresolved questions and prepare the user-facing decision brief.

### 6. User authority

The user controls consequential preference decisions and canonical promotion. No seat may self-promote its output.

## Required research distinctions

Keep separate:

- cost of initial account creation;
- cost of ageing and maintaining history;
- cost of credible behavioural variation;
- cost of independent social and transactional graphs;
- cost imposed by platform controls and detection;
- cost of recovery after correlated compromise;
- attacker fixed costs, marginal costs and economies of scale;
- monetary, time, human-attention and risk-adjusted costs.

## Candidate model families

1. **Linear:** constant marginal cost per credible identity after setup.
2. **Sublinear:** automation, shared infrastructure or purchased histories create economies of scale.
3. **Superlinear:** coherence, independence, graph separation, detection avoidance or correlated risk make marginal cost rise with scale.
4. **Regime-dependent:** sublinear or linear at small scale, followed by threshold-driven superlinearity or catastrophic correlated loss.

No family is privileged in advance.

## Safety boundary

This is defensive and analytical research. Do not provide operational instructions for creating fraudulent identities, bypassing verification, evading detection, acquiring credentials or abusing real platforms. Use aggregate evidence and synthetic models.
